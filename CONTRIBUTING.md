# Contributing to Predictive History

Thank you for considering a contribution. This repository is a source-disciplined study companion for Professor Jiang's Predictive History corpus, and we welcome issues and pull requests that make it clearer, more traceable, and more useful for serious students.

## Core Principles

- Accuracy and traceability come first.
- Keep source material, summary, interpretation, prediction, and disagreement clearly separated.
- Representation is not endorsement.
- Help readers verify what was actually said, when it was said, and in what context.

## Contribution Rules

- Preserve stable source IDs when known, such as `geo-01`, `vi-16`, or `es-13`.
- Include original source URLs, publication dates, and video timestamps where practical.
- Distinguish clearly between:
  - original source text
  - neutral summary
  - interpretation or analysis
  - predictions
  - counter-readings or disagreements
- Do not add unsupported claims or speculation.
- Do not import private notes, internal drafts, or unpublished material.
- Keep the tone respectful and scholarly.

## Rights and Source Text Policy

By submitting a contribution, you confirm that you have the legal right to share the material you add.

Do not paste large transcript blocks, full essays, paywalled content, or extensive copyrighted text unless the repository has an approved policy for that source. Short, clearly attributed excerpts for study purposes are generally acceptable. When in doubt, link to the original source and summarize in your own words.

Longer curated transcripts or excerpt collections require prior discussion and rights review.

## How to Contribute

### Corrections and Improvements

Use issues or pull requests for:

- transcript corrections
- source metadata fixes
- broken or outdated links
- annotation disagreements
- new source suggestions
- typos, formatting issues, or clarity improvements

See [docs/corrections-policy.md](docs/corrections-policy.md) for the detailed correction workflow.

### New Annotations or Content

- Follow the [annotation template](docs/annotation-block.md)
- Use the established [source status vocabulary](docs/source-status.md)
- Place new material in the appropriate folder structure, as described in [docs/repo-map.md](docs/repo-map.md)

## Review Expectations

Maintainers may ask you to:

- add or improve timestamps
- shorten long quotations
- clarify whether a note is summary or interpretation
- add appropriate uncertainty or status labels
- move broad disagreements into a dedicated counter-reading section

Our goal is not to be overly restrictive. We want the repository to be useful, honest, and intellectually rigorous.
