# For Professor Jiang

## Status

This repository is an unofficial, independent, and corrigible study companion for Professor Jiang's Predictive History corpus. It is not an official archive, not a substitute for the original videos or essays, and not endorsed by Professor Jiang unless explicitly stated.

Representation is not endorsement. The repository documents and studies claims in the corpus; it does not automatically affirm every claim, prediction, interpretation, or framing.

## Purpose

Predictive History is built to help serious students study with source discipline. The public surfaces are organized around source items, source transcripts, commentaries, civ-ph placement cards, claims, concepts, counter-readings, prediction follow-ups, and return paths back to the original source.

The intended student value is practical:

- find the relevant source item quickly
- distinguish source text, summary, interpretation, and disagreement
- see review status and rights limits clearly
- follow chapter routes without losing source context
- report corrections without turning the repo into a frozen interpretation

## How This Can Help The Course

The repository should function as a structured mirror for the course. It can surface patterns that are useful for teaching, revision, and student support without asking the course to adopt the repo's framing.

Course improvement signals include:

- recurring student questions
- confusion points in a lecture, source item, or route
- missing bibliography or support
- correction needs
- prediction follow-ups and falsifier tracking
- places where a bridge, caveat, clarification, or sequel could help students

The strongest claim here is educational: serious students benefit from source discipline, chapter routing, claims, counter-readings, and return paths.

## Correction And Supersession

Corrections are welcome, especially when they come from the original source, Professor Jiang, or the course team. See [docs/corrections-policy.md](docs/corrections-policy.md) for the correction standard and review outcomes, and [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) for the contribution path.

Professor Jiang or the course team may recommend, correct, ignore, or supersede this repository. Any correction that clarifies the source record, rights boundary, review status, or student-facing interpretation should be treated as high-priority governance feedback.

## What This Repo Will Not Claim

This repository will not claim:

- official status
- definitive interpretation
- implied endorsement
- ownership of Professor Jiang's course
- recognition as a prerequisite for continuing the work
