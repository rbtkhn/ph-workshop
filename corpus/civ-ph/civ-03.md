# civ-03 - civ-ph Placement

## Where This Sits

This chapter continues the early Civilization arc by treating religious imagination as a core human faculty rather than a secondary belief system. It sits near the beginning of the sequence because it helps explain how humans turn uncertainty, fear, awe, and perception into shared symbolic worlds.

## Reading Posture

Read this as a theory-of-mind chapter for civilization. The useful question is not only what early people believed, but how imagination created the conditions for ritual authority, communal meaning, and social coordination.

## Historical Pressure Points

- Religious imagination gives shape to unseen causes and invisible worlds.
- Shared symbols can organize people before states, bureaucracies, or formal doctrines exist.
- Vision, myth, and ritual help communities make uncertainty socially usable.
- The chapter strengthens the bridge from prehistoric symbolic life to later organized religion.
