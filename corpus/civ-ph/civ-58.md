# civ-58 - civ-ph Placement
## Where This Sits
This chapter begins the final three-class capstone. It places the nation-state after religion, capitalism, reason, modernism, and alienation, then uses French and German nationalism to prepare the Soviet and American Empire chapters.
## Reading Posture
Read it as the setup chapter for the course ending. Its placement is strong because it explains why the nation becomes a modern object of faith and sacrifice, but its race, eugenics, Holocaust, immigration, and current-politics claims need tight limits.
## Historical Pressure Points
- Nationalism is framed as a political-religious solution to alienation and crisis of faith.
- Industrialization, bourgeois property, urbanization, print, and standardization make the nation-state workable.
- French civic nationalism is contrasted with German romantic-cultural nationalism.
- Race science, eugenics, imperialism, fascism, totalitarianism, and Pax Americana turn the chapter into the final-course bridge.
