# gb-02 - civ-ph Placement
## Where This Sits
This lecture moves from the course frame into Homer as the invention of a human world in which heroic action, mortality, speech, and memory become readable as literature.
## Reading Posture
Read it as a literary-origin chapter that turns the canon from an abstract promise into a concrete encounter with Homeric humanity.
## Historical Pressure Points
- Homer becomes the first major test of what great books can preserve about human action and self-understanding.
- The lecture connects epic poetry to memory, heroism, mortality, and the formation of the human interior.
- It sets up later Homeric returns through the Odyssey, Virgil, Dante, and anti-Homeric reversals.
## Limits of the Frame
This entry is in review. Claims about Homer inventing the human, oral culture, Greek priority, and literary origin should remain lecture interpretations pending external literary and historical review.
