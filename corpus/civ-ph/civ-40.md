# civ-40 - civ-ph Placement

## Where This Sits

This chapter reopens Europe after the Islam-China-Mongol pivot by making the Crusades and medieval church power the entry point into Renaissance, Reformation, and Scientific Revolution material. It places the reader inside a world where salvation, hierarchy, wealth, violence, and afterlife expectation are institutional forces, not background beliefs.

## Reading Posture

Read it as a strong bridge chapter with ethical guardrails. The lecture is not simply summarizing Crusades chronology; it is building the church-and-empire pressure system that later European ruptures respond to.

## Historical Pressure Points

- Catholic authority is framed as an imperial bureaucracy after Rome.
- Church wealth and salvation control become central institutional pressures.
- Heresy, orthodoxy, purgatory, and ritual organize political as well as religious life.
- The scapegoating discussion requires special care because it describes anti-Jewish violence as a political mechanism.

## Limits of the Frame

This entry is in review after initial commentary analysis. The placement is strong, but Crusades causation, Catholic theology, claims about church wealth, and the explanation of anti-Jewish violence must remain bounded and non-exculpatory until external review.

## Return Path

Return here when later chapters discuss Renaissance rebellion, Protestant criticism of church mediation, secular authority, European violence, scapegoating, or the conversion of religious worldview into institutional power.
