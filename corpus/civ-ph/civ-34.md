# civ-34 - civ-ph Placement

## Where This Sits

This chapter builds the western Roman afterlife. After Byzantium preserves Rome in the East, the Holy Roman Empire becomes the western fiction that lets Charlemagne, the papacy, local princes, and Augustinian imagination create a loose sacred-political order.

## Reading Posture

Read it as a strong legitimacy chapter. The key is not whether the Holy Roman Empire literally was holy, Roman, or imperial, but how that claim helped organize a divided Europe.

## Historical Pressure Points

- Charlemagne and Pope Leo III turn coronation into a legitimacy exchange.
- European geography makes unity difficult and symbolic authority unusually important.
- The Catholic Church functions as a distributed infrastructure of authority, memory, and social mobility.
- Augustine's City of God is read as a deep imaginative source for medieval political order.
