# civ-06 - civ-ph Placement

## Where This Sits

This chapter brings the Civilization sequence into systemic collapse. The Bronze Age world becomes a case study in elite overproduction, rent-seeking, debt, institutional fragility, and the way complex societies can fail when their ruling strata multiply beyond the system that sustains them.

## Reading Posture

Read this as a civilizational failure chapter. The key is to notice how elite incentives, social pressure, and institutional complexity interact, while keeping elite overproduction as an interpretive model rather than a settled replacement for all collapse explanations.

## Historical Pressure Points

- Elite overproduction turns social success into systemic stress.
- Bronze Age collapse provides a model for fragility inside complexity.
- Rent-seeking, debt, and palace extraction become internal sources of instability.
- Institutional competition can weaken the order it claims to preserve.
- The chapter creates a bridge between ancient collapse and predictive-history style diagnosis.
