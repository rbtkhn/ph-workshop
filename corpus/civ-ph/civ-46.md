# civ-46 - civ-ph Placement

## Where This Sits

This chapter opens the French Revolution sequence by turning reason into a civilizational force. The Enlightenment is framed as a new worldview that competes with older religion and begins moving sacred commitment into the nation-state.

## Reading Posture

Read it as a strong hinge into revolutionary modernity. The chapter is broad by design: it compresses religion, worldview, Enlightenment, Rousseau, civil religion, and nationalism into a frame that later Robespierre and Napoleon chapters need to test.

## Historical Pressure Points

- Worldview succession organizes the lecture from animism through reason.
- Reason, debate, and progress are treated as Enlightenment foundations.
- Rousseau and the Social Contract become central to civil religion.
- The nation-state begins to absorb religious commitment and sacrifice.
