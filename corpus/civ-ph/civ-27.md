# civ-27 - civ-ph Placement

## Where This Sits

This chapter sits where imperial Christianity becomes a long-duration institution. Augustine is framed as the thinker who lets the Church survive Rome's failure by claiming a spiritual authority above ordinary historical collapse.

## Reading Posture

Read it as a strong institutional chapter, but one with unusually sharp interpretive edges. The lecture is not only summarizing Augustine; it is testing how Augustine's rhetoric can legitimate obedience, priestly authority, and a Christian end-of-history order.

## Historical Pressure Points

- End-of-history claims connect David, Augustus, Constantine, Theodosius, and Augustine.
- Rome's sack creates a crisis for imperial Christianity.
- City of God separates earthly power from spiritual authority.
- Confessions and City of God are read as tools for obedience, sin, and Church legitimacy.
