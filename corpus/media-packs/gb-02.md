# Great Books #2: Homer and the Invention of the Human Media Pack

## How To Use This Pack

Use this pack before reading the transcript or commentary. It gives students a visual and documentary doorway into the lecture while keeping source claims, interpretation, and rights status separate. This is a curated draft awaiting human search and curation.

## Entry Object

### 01. Bust known as Homer

- **Type:** portrait
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Homer_British_Museum.jpg
- **Rights:** external_link_only
- **What to notice:** The blind-bard image asks students to treat Homer as a cultural memory figure as much as an individual author.
- **Limit:** Ancient portrait types are conventional and not reliable likenesses.

## Context Anchors

### 04. Troy archaeological site

- **Type:** place
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Troy
- **Rights:** external_link_only
- **What to notice:** Troy grounds the epic in contested layers of archaeology, memory, and legend.
- **Limit:** The site's layers do not straightforwardly prove the Iliad's narrative.

## Primary Objects And Texts

### 02. Iliad manuscript page

- **Type:** manuscript
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Iliad_manuscripts
- **Rights:** external_link_only
- **What to notice:** A manuscript page reminds the reader that oral epic later became textual inheritance.
- **Limit:** Manuscript evidence belongs to later transmission, not the original performance setting.

### 03. Achilles tending Patroclus

- **Type:** artwork
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Akhilleus_Patroklos_Antikensammlung_Berlin_F2278.jpg
- **Rights:** external_link_only
- **What to notice:** The scene centers vulnerability and friendship inside heroic violence, a key close-reading pressure.
- **Limit:** The vase interprets the story visually and should not replace the poem's language.

## Comparison Objects

### 05. Catalogue of Ships map

- **Type:** map
- **Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalogue_of_Ships
- **Rights:** external_link_only
- **What to notice:** The catalogue shows epic as a geography of peoples, alliances, and remembered names.
- **Limit:** The map is a modern reconstruction of poetic material.
