# Civilization #29: Dante's Divine Comedy and the Liberation of the Human Imagination Media Pack

## How To Use This Pack

Use this pack before reading the transcript or commentary. It gives students a visual and documentary doorway into the lecture while keeping source claims, interpretation, and rights status separate. This is a curated draft awaiting human search and curation.

## Entry Object

### 01. Dante portrait by Botticelli

- **Type:** portrait
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Sandro_Botticelli_-_Portrait_of_Dante_Alighieri.jpg
- **Rights:** external_link_only
- **What to notice:** The portrait places Dante as a historical author whose imaginative authority becomes a civilizational force.
- **Limit:** A Renaissance portrait is an afterimage, not a contemporary record of Dante.

## Context Anchors

### 02. Dante and the three realms

- **Type:** diagram
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Dante_Domenico_di_Michelino_Duomo_Florence.jpg
- **Rights:** external_link_only
- **What to notice:** The image helps students see the Comedy as a mapped cosmos linking city, exile, theology, and poetic journey.
- **Limit:** The painting visualizes later reception and should not replace the poem.

### 04. Florence city view

- **Type:** place
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Florence_in_the_Middle_Ages
- **Rights:** external_link_only
- **What to notice:** Florence gives the poem its civic and factional pressure: paradise and hell are written from political exile.
- **Limit:** Category-level media need item-level review before use as evidence.

## Primary Objects And Texts

### 03. Botticelli map of hell

- **Type:** diagram
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Botticelli_Chart_of_Hell.jpg
- **Rights:** external_link_only
- **What to notice:** The chart turns moral order into spatial order, making Dante's imagination visible as architecture.
- **Limit:** It is a later interpretive diagram, not Dante's own drawing.

### 05. Manuscripts of the Divine Comedy

- **Type:** manuscript
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Manuscripts_of_Divina_Commedia
- **Rights:** external_link_only
- **What to notice:** Manuscript witnesses show the Comedy becoming a shared European memory object.
- **Limit:** Individual manuscripts differ by date, region, illustration, and textual tradition.
