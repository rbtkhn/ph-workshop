# Predictive History Museum Exhibit: Great Books #9: Dante (Re-Upload with Audio Fixed)

## How To Use This Exhibit

Use this exhibit before reading the transcript or commentary. It gives students a curated artifact doorway into the lecture: objects, texts, maps, places, diagrams, music, speeches, documents, performances, and pressure systems, while keeping source claims, interpretation, and rights status separate. This is a curated draft awaiting human search and curation.

## Entrance Artifact

### 01. Dante holding the Comedy

- **Type:** artwork
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Dante_Domenico_di_Michelino_Duomo_Florence.jpg
- **Rights:** external_link_only
- **What to notice:** This image frames Dante as poet, exile, and maker of a world students can learn to read spatially.
- **What this cannot prove:** It is later civic commemoration, not a direct source for Dante's intention.

## Context Room

### 04. Medieval cosmology diagram

- **Type:** diagram
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Medieval_cosmology
- **Rights:** external_link_only
- **What to notice:** Cosmological diagrams make the poem's ordered universe easier to enter.
- **What this cannot prove:** Generic cosmology diagrams may not match Dante's exact poetic architecture.

## Primary Artifacts And Texts

### 02. Gustave Dore Inferno illustration

- **Type:** artwork
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Illustrations_to_Dante%27s_Divine_Comedy_by_Gustave_Dor%C3%A9
- **Rights:** external_link_only
- **What to notice:** Doré shows how later readers visualized terror, judgment, and moral atmosphere in Dante.
- **What this cannot prove:** Nineteenth-century illustration can overpower the poem if treated as primary meaning.

### 03. Botticelli Paradiso drawing

- **Type:** artwork
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Drawings_of_Divine_Comedy_by_Sandro_Botticelli
- **Rights:** external_link_only
- **What to notice:** Botticelli's drawings help students see line, ascent, and order as interpretive problems.
- **What this cannot prove:** The drawings are a Renaissance reading of Dante, not neutral illustration.

## Comparison Artifacts

### 05. Beatrice and Dante imagery

- **Type:** artwork
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Dante_and_Beatrice
- **Rights:** external_link_only
- **What to notice:** Beatrice imagery foregrounds love as guidance, judgment, distance, and transformation.
- **What this cannot prove:** Later romantic imagery can soften Dante's theological and philosophical stakes.
