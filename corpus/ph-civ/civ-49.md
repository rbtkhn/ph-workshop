# civ-49 - PH-CIV Placement

## Where This Sits

This chapter opens the course's final Anglo-American Empire section by placing the Dutch Republic between Spanish extraction and British expansion. The Dutch Golden Age becomes a bridge from early modern trade and capitalism into middle-class life, art, domesticity, and later imperial habits.

## Reading Posture

Read it as a strong seedbed chapter. Its force is comparative: Spain, the Dutch Republic, Britain, and America are positioned as different ways wealth, religion, commerce, and social class can organize power.

## Historical Pressure Points

- Spanish gold and silver are framed as intensifying a feudal Catholic order.
- The Dutch Republic is treated as a commercial, capitalist, middle-class alternative.
- Art and domestic interiors become evidence for a new social imagination.
- Dutch forms are routed forward into British and American imperial development.

## Limits of the Frame

This entry is in review after initial commentary analysis. The placement is strong, but Spain-Dutch comparison, Catholic and feudal claims, capitalism origins, middle-class psychology, and art-to-society readings must remain bounded pending external review.

## Return Path

Return here when later chapters discuss Britain, America, capitalism, corporations, middle-class culture, domesticity, art, hypocrisy, trade, or the commercial roots of Anglo-American power.
