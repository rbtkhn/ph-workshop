# civ-29 - PH-CIV Placement

## Where This Sits

This chapter sits as the first half of the Dante capstone. After the course moves through imperial monotheism, Augustine, and Islam, Dante is introduced as the figure who reopens love, imagination, and human agency inside a Christian cosmos.

## Reading Posture

Read it as a strong literary-civilizational chapter. The lecture is doing close reading, theology, and historical placement at once, so keep Dante's textual argument distinct from the broader claim that the Commedia seeds later European revolutions.

## Historical Pressure Points

- Dante is placed against Augustine's doctrine of sin, obedience, and self-negation.
- Mary becomes a sign of human nobility and love rather than simple inherited sin.
- Paradox and structure are treated as poetic devices that remake the mind.
- Dante's vision of God is framed as imagination meeting cosmic unity.
