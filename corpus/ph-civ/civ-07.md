# civ-07 - PH-CIV Placement

## Where This Sits

This chapter shifts into Greek civilizational memory through Homer and the Iliad. It sits where myth, violence, aristocratic honor, and collective identity begin to turn inherited rupture into a literary foundation for Greek civilization.

## Reading Posture

Read this as a literary-civilizational bridge. The Iliad is not only a text to interpret; in this sequence it becomes a memory device for how a people imagines courage, status, grief, and order.

## Historical Pressure Points

- Epic poetry preserves social ideals and wounds across generations.
- Greek civilization is approached through heroic memory before formal philosophy or democracy.
- The Iliad frames violence as both glory and civilizational burden.
- Literature becomes an archive of political and moral formation.
