# civ-07 - PH-CIV Placement

## Where This Sits

This chapter shifts into Greek civilizational memory through polis, alphabet, Homer, and the Iliad. It sits where post-collapse poverty and decentralization become sources of political, linguistic, and literary invention.

## Reading Posture

Read this as a literary-civilizational bridge. The Iliad is not only a text to interpret; in this sequence it becomes a memory device for how a people imagines courage, status, grief, empathy, thought, and order.

## Historical Pressure Points

- Epic poetry preserves social ideals and wounds across generations.
- Alphabetic writing changes who can think, remember, and circulate speech.
- Greek civilization is approached through heroic memory before formal philosophy or democracy.
- The Iliad frames violence as both glory and civilizational burden.
- Literature becomes an archive of political and moral formation.
