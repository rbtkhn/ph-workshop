# civ-17 - PH-CIV Placement

## Where This Sits

This chapter closes the Macedon-to-Rome hinge by asking how Rome narrates its own soul after Augustus. The transition from Homer to Virgil is a transition from Greek imaginative inheritance to Roman duty, destiny, and imperial memory.

## Reading Posture

Read it as a literary-civilizational placement rather than a simple poetry comparison. The Aeneid matters because Rome needs a story equal to its new imperial structure.

## Historical Pressure Points

- Augustus stabilizes power through army, guard, and Egypt's fiscal role.
- Aeneas becomes a founding-memory figure for Roman destiny.
- Homer and Virgil are contrasted as different civilizational imaginations.
- The chapter points forward to Egyptian eternity and later Christian inheritance.
