## Where This Sits

This chapter sits as the second half of the Dante capstone and the close of the first-semester arc. Dante is framed not only as a Christian poet but as the figure who can replace Virgil inside European memory.

## Reading Posture

Read it as a strong literary-memory chapter. The lecture uses close reading of Virgil, Dido, Cato, Statius, and Beatrice to argue that Dante performs a subtle replacement of the older epic mind rather than a simple critique of it.

- Poetry is treated as a memorized structure that shapes subconscious thought.
- Virgil is honored as guide and exposed as unreliable.
- Love is reframed away from possession and toward trust, freedom, and selfless regard.
- Dante's poetic revolution is linked to later Reformation and scientific doubt claims.
