# Secret History #11: Dawn of the Human Imagination

## Where This Sits

This Secret History unit supplies a pre-Homeric imagination root for the literary spine: ritual, care, cave art, and story become the conditions that make later epic and scriptural memory intelligible.

## Reading Posture

Read this as a support node for the literary arc, not as a complete theory of prehistory. Its value is in showing why imagination, empathy, and symbolic life matter before named authors appear.

## Historical Pressure Points

  - Darwinian selection and its misuse in social theory
  - Cave art, ritual, shamanic imagination, and early symbolic practice
  - Care, disability, dependency, and empathy as civilizational facts
  - Milton, Beethoven, and later creators as echoes of older imaginative capacities

## Limits of the Frame

This entry is in review and treats prehistoric, evolutionary, disability, race, and religious claims as lecture interpretations pending external scholarly review.
