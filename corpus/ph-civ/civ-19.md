# civ-19 - PH-CIV Placement

## Where This Sits

This chapter places Mesopotamia beside Egypt as a different Bronze Age answer to death, power, and order. Instead of entering through a monument, the reader enters through myth, city diversity, creation story, and Gilgamesh's failed quest for immortality.

## Reading Posture

Read it comparatively. The lecture uses Egypt as the counterpoint, but the goal is not to rank civilizations; it is to see how different geographies and political forms produce different mythic answers to mortality and rule.

## Historical Pressure Points

- Mesopotamia is framed through diversity and city-state plurality.
- Enuma Elish and Egyptian kingship myth become contrasting order stories.
- Gilgamesh turns mortality into the central civilizational question.
- The chapter prepares Indus as a peaceful and egalitarian countercase.

## Limits of the Frame

This entry is in review after initial commentary analysis. The frame is strong because Mesopotamia is a major comparative pole, but myth interpretation, Sumerian origins, and social-evolution readings need final philological and historical checking.
