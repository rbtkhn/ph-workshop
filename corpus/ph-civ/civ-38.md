# civ-38 - PH-CIV Placement

## Where This Sits

This chapter pulls the Civilization spine into China as a test of the course's models. It asks whether the same ideas about competition, bureaucracy, creativity, empire, and civilizational decline can explain Chinese history as well as European history.

## Reading Posture

Read it as a strong model-testing chapter. The lecture is valuable because it pushes the course beyond Western examples, but its claims about Chinese creativity, stagnation, Confucianism, and examinations should be held with visible limits.

## Historical Pressure Points

- China is treated as a historically creative civilization that made modernity possible.
- Warring States competition and the Hundred Schools become sources of intellectual formation.
- Confucianism is read as bureaucratic legitimation.
- The imperial examination system is framed as status distribution and center-local control.

## Limits of the Frame

This entry is in review after initial commentary analysis. The placement is strong because the chapter tests the course models on China, but the stagnation thesis, Song-era inflection, Confucianism-as-bureaucracy reading, and examination-control interpretation require careful external review.
