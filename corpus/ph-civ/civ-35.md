# civ-35 - PH-CIV Placement

## Where This Sits

This chapter begins the Viking/Norse pair after the Holy Roman Empire. It moves from Rome and Catholic legitimacy into the northern borderland cultures that the course presents as unexpectedly central to Western civilization.

## Reading Posture

Read it as a strong borderland-and-influence chapter. The lecture is making a large placement claim for the Vikings, so the reader should track both the evidence of movement and the limits of the proposed fifth-pillar frame.

## Historical Pressure Points

- The Vikings are proposed as a fifth pillar of Western civilization.
- Raiding, trade, settlement, exploration, Normandy, England, and Russia become linked effects of Viking movement.
- Community, exile, and local belonging are treated as core premodern identity structures.
- The chapter prepares the shift from Viking history to Norse memory and worldview.

## Limits of the Frame

This entry is in review after initial commentary analysis. The placement is strong because the chapter opens the Viking pair and makes the major fifth-pillar claim, but Viking influence, Russia/Normandy/England pathways, premodern tolerance, and race-history claims require external review before final synthesis.
