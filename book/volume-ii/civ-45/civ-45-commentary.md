## Layer 1 - Neutral Summary

The lecture asks how Europe, after centuries of weakness following Rome, conquered much of the world after about 1700. Jiang returns to a recurring course model: the nature of the military determines the nature of the political system.

The chapter reviews Sparta, Athens, Macedonia, and Rome as examples of military form shaping oligarchy, democracy, monarchy, and republic. It then turns to gunpowder as a new military form requiring engineers, professional armies, fiscal capacity, centralized state power, towns, and industry.

The ending extends the model into schooling and industrial discipline. Jiang argues that modern schooling descends from military needs for obedience and separation from family, then becomes useful for industry and factory labor.
