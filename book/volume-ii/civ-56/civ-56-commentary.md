## Layer 1 - Neutral Summary
The lecture begins by placing Marx after Kant and Hegel. Jiang presents Marx as taking Hegelian Geist, dialectic, and master-slave logic and inverting them into material history, class struggle, and the promised movement from capitalism into communism.

The middle of the lecture treats Marx as both a powerful diagnostician of capitalism and an unreliable prophet of history. Capitalism is described as consuming, consolidating, imperial, and alienating, with alienation from labor, self, nature, and humanity functioning as the strongest part of Marx's account.

The ending argues that Marx got history and psychology wrong because religion, God, and status matter more than economics and class. Communism, capitalism, China, Russia, Korea, education, and Freud are then used to carry the Civilization spine from German idealism into modern psychology and ideology.
