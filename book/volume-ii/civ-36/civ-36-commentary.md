## Layer 1 - Neutral Summary

The lecture continues the Viking pair by shifting from historical overview to worldview and cultural system. It begins with a caution: the Vikings were intentionally oral, later Christianized, and often remembered through hostile or later sources, so Norse culture must be reconstructed from limited and biased evidence.

Jiang contrasts oral and literary cultures. The lecture argues that oral tradition preserves memory through performance, intimacy, play, repetition, and imagination, while literary culture makes language stable but also public, watchful, and shame-producing. This distinction becomes the core of the chapter.

The conclusion links Norse memory to larger literary questions. Jiang asks whether Homer, Dante, and Shakespeare would have been possible without oral tradition and whether civilization itself makes people less creative by making them more ashamed, observed, and constrained. The next chapter is explicitly marked as a move to the Abbasid caliphate.
