# Tolstoy Lens

The Tolstoy Lens is the bounded commentary form of the existing Tolstoy Question. It asks how visible actors relate to deeper pressure fields without replacing Jiang's argument or turning the repository into a Tolstoyan house style.

Use this standard only for source IDs listed in [registries/causation-lenses.yaml](../registries/causation-lenses.yaml). The registry is the source of truth for visible actors, pressure fields, causation questions, and cautions.

## Jiang / Tolstoy Choreography

The commentary should move in two distinct steps:

1. **Jiang Lens**: preserve the lecture's argument on its own terms. State what Jiang argues, which claims are explicit, which claims are interpretive, and what remains in review.
2. **Tolstoy Lens**: ask a second-order causation question beside Jiang's argument. When the lecture names an actor, institution, empire, or crisis that appears to make history, ask what deeper pressure field made that actor historically possible.

The Tolstoy Lens does not overrule Jiang's interpretation. It asks a second-order causation question beside it.

Place the section after counter-readings and before synthesis or open issues. Do not renumber existing commentary layers.

## Voice Rules

- Keep the prose plain, reflective, and analytic.
- Do not write the commentary in Tolstoy's voice.
- Do not use parody, pastiche, sermon, or literary performance.
- Do not turn pressure fields into fate.
- Do not say the visible actor does not matter.
- Do not use system pressure to excuse violence or erase moral responsibility.

## Acceptance Standard

A Tolstoy Lens section is acceptable only if it:

- preserves Jiang's lecture before adding the causation question
- identifies the visible actor or apparent historical agent
- names the deeper pressure field
- preserves agency while resisting great-man simplification
- states where the lens supports Jiang
- states where the lens complicates Jiang
- includes a falsifier or limit
- returns readers to the source item, civ-ph placement card, or Tolstoy Question corridor

## Boundaries

The lens is a parallel reading layer, not a correction layer by default. It can support Jiang, complicate Jiang, or reveal where a lecture risks overpersonalizing causation, but it should not present itself as the real interpretation above the course.
